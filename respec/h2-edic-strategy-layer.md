#  EDIC Strategy layer
## Motivation

This view establishes the "Why" behind the EDIC, defining the guiding principles for its design and evolution.

### EDIC Strategic Drivers
Four strategic drivers explain why the EDIC is being established and what challenges it needs to address:
*   **Urban Complexity:** Cross-sectoral challenges require an integral approach.
*   **Resource Constraints:** Municipalities lack the capacity and skills to maintain LDTs independently.
*	**Intellectual Property Management:** Handling ownership and rights across partners
*	**Multi-layer Governance:** Coordinating across different levels of authority

### Core Values (Digital Decade 2030)
The EDIC should help establish and comply to the values of the Digital Decade 2030:

* People at the Centre
* Participation
* Freedom of Choice
* Safety and Security
* Sustainability
* Solidarity and Inclusion

These values form the normative foundation of the entire architecture, both of the EDIC and the reference architecture for LDTs.

### Goals and Objectives
*   **Common Infrastructure:** Connecting local digital twins across Europe to form the foundation of the EU CitiVERSE.
*   **Digital Sovereignty:** Promoting European control over data and AI in urban management.
*	**Self-Sustainability Models:** Ensuring the EDIC can sustain itself over time
*	**Capacity building:** Enabling and supporting cities and regions to succesfully adopt, use and scale Digital Twins and immersive Technologies

**eGovERA Capabilities:**
The EDIC must help establish the eGovERA capabilities and become a go-to organisation for its members on:
* Digital Twin
* Knowledge Management
* Data Management
* Artificial Intelligence

### Guiding Principles 
*   **Interoperability by Design:** All services must combat fragmentation through open standards (e.g., OGC, MIMs).
*   **Public Value Focus:** Technology must serve the well-being of citizens and evidence-based decision-making.
*   **Lawfullness:** Compliance with EU rules and regulations.

## Capabilities and Enablement

Capabilities represent the "link" between strategic goals and the operational play of the organization. Capabilities are the 'things' the organisation (EDIC) is, or must be, able to do to reach its strategic goals.

### Core Business Capabilities
*   **Ecosystem Orchestration:** The ability to coordinate multiple Member States, knowledge and technology providers.
*   **Data Governance & Stewardship:** Managing trusted data sharing through Data Spaces (e.g., DS4SSCC, SIMPL) .
*   **Interoperability Management:** Profiling and promoting technical and procedural standards to reduce fragmentation.

### Organizational Competencies
*   **Strategic Advisory:** Helping cities navigate EU funding and digital transformation roadmaps.
*   **Validation & Benchmarking:** Establishing frameworks to test the quality and reliability of LDT componenents and approaches.
